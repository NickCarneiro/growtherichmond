Wow, it's already more than halfway through February! We organized more than 100 neighbors to sign a petition supporting great local business Orson's Belly, attended the CA ABC hearing, and though they still won't know the outcome, went to celebrate a great local business, among other actions this month.

 

We're more than halfway through the vote-by-mail period here in San Francisco and Election Day is 2 weeks away. If you are interested in how you can help, there's still time to get involved with a campaign, and plenty of other stuff going on in the neighborhood right now.

What's Happening In The Richmond Right Now?
  • The Planning Association for the Richmond (PAR) will be holding their first meeting of 2020 on February 19th from 7 - 9 pm at the Richmond Rec Center (251 18th Ave). They will be featuring speakers on the ballot measures on the March primary ballot. RSVP here.
  • If you are not a member of PAR, consider joining today!
  • There are only two weeks left to support Richmond District residents and Grow The Richmond members Nadia Rahman and Jane Natoli in their run for DCCC! Check out their campaigns and tell your friends about their campaigns and their slate.
  • They will be hosting a lit drop on Saturday at Lucky Ocean Cafe at 1501 Ocean Ave at 9:45 am. Details here.
  • Sunday, we'll be supporting them at Blue Fog Market at 2567 Gough at 9:45 am. Details here.
  • Check out the rest of the YIMBY-endorsed candidates here! Give it a share on social media and contribute to them here.

In the news
  • In case you missed everything that's been happening with Orson's Belly, Hoodline has you covered about how we bury our small businesses under red tape.
  • Mayor Breed has put forth a bold proposal to try and get more homes built in San Francisco. Stay tuned for ways you can help support it

Other events coming up
